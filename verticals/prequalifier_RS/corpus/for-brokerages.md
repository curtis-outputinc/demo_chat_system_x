# For brokers and brokerages (broker-side talking points)

This file is what the assistant speaks to when a broker or owner is
exploring the demo in broker-side mode. It is value-talk, not regulatory
copy.

## What this prequalifier does for a brokerage

It runs a real structured prequalification interview on the brokerage's
website, around the clock, in the brokerage's own voice, drawing only from
what the brokerage has put into its knowledge base. It is calibrated to
stay inside the lines a licensed broker needs to stay inside: no rate
quotes, no approval promises, no lender recommendations, no sensitive
document collection in chat.

## What is different from a generic website chatbot

- It does not just answer questions. It actively walks the borrower
  through a prequalification: goal, property, mortgage size, timeline,
  income shape, credit range, debts, and anything unusual about the file.
- It reads the file as it goes and quietly maps it to a likely lending
  bucket: conventional A, alternative (B-lender / MIC), or private.
- It scores the file on the back end, where the borrower never sees the
  score. The broker sees a ranked list of files worth their time.
- It hands the file off as a clean synopsis the broker can read in ten
  seconds before picking up the phone.

## What the broker gets back

For every borrower who completes (or partially completes) the
prequalification, the brokerage sees:

- First name and phone for callback. No surnames, no email, no addresses
  unless the broker chooses to extend the schema.
- A read of the file: goal, property snapshot, mortgage size or down
  payment, timeline and any urgency flags, employment and income shape,
  credit range, debts, and anything unusual.
- A bucket read (conventional, alternative, or private) with the
  reasoning visible.
- A score reflecting how complete and how strong the file is.
- The full conversation transcript, kept private and used only for the
  brokerage's own dashboard.

The brokerage also gets the same insight every other vertical here gets:
how many visitors started a prequalification, where they dropped off, what
questions came up that the prequalifier could not answer, and which pages
on the broker's site drive the most starts.

## After-hours coverage and alt / private intake

This is where the prequalifier earns its keep. Strong borrowers find
brokers' sites at 11pm, on weekends, and during commutes. Alt and private
borrowers in particular often shop late, because they have been told no
elsewhere and they want to feel something out before talking to a person.
The prequalifier captures those files when the broker's team is not
available, runs them through the interview, scores them, and queues them
for the morning.

## Privacy posture

Conversations are kept private and secure and are used only for the
brokerage's own analysis. The prequalifier does not forward conversations
or contact details outside the brokerage's own dashboard. It does not
collect identification, full bank statements, tax returns, pay stubs,
credit reports, full SSN or SIN, or account numbers in chat. Those move
through the broker's secure workflow after the first call. The brokerage
controls how long borrower records are retained.

## What it does not do

The prequalifier does not quote rates, guarantee approval, recommend
specific lenders, give tax or legal advice, or make qualifying decisions.
Brokers testing this should expect it to redirect those questions to the
broker every time. That is intentional and what makes it safe to put on a
licensed broker's website.
