# CMHC Newcomers program (Federal Canada)

Overlay on the CMHC homeownership base. Use this file only for the
newcomers-specific rules; everything else (LTV ceilings, down payment
tiers, DSR limits, MQR, credit floor, amortization rules, premium
framework, property location) comes from
`cmhc-mortgage-loan-insurance.md`.

## Source URL

- CMHC Newcomers program page:
  https://www.cmhc-schl.gc.ca/professionals/project-funding-and-mortgage-financing/mortgage-loan-insurance/mortgage-loan-insurance-homeownership-programs/newcomers

## Eligibility by immigration status

### Permanent residents

- Access to all CMHC homeowner mortgage loan insurance products.
- No minimum residency period required in Canada.
- May purchase 1-4 unit properties under the standard homeowner
  framework.

### Non-permanent residents (work permit holders)

- Must be legally authorized to work in Canada (e.g. valid work
  permit).
- Limited to 1-4 unit properties with at least one owner-occupied
  unit.
- Must be exempt from the Prohibition on the Purchase of Residential
  Property by Non-Canadians Act and the accompanying regulations.
- NOT eligible for non-traditional down payment sources (only
  traditional sources: savings, sale of property, non-repayable family
  gift).

## Credit history for newcomers

The minimum credit score of 600 (for at least one borrower or
guarantor) from the base file still applies. Where the borrower has
limited or no Canadian credit history, CMHC may consider alternative
methods of establishing creditworthiness, including:

- International credit reports from the borrower's country of origin.
- A letter of reference from the borrower's financial institution in
  their country of origin.
- Other alternative documentation accepted on a case-by-case basis.

The lender confirms what alternative documentation is acceptable for
the specific borrower's situation.

## Down payment

Standard CMHC rules apply (5% on first $500K plus 10% above for homes
under $1.5M, 20% on small rental, etc.).

Sources:

- Traditional sources are always acceptable: savings, sale of
  property, non-repayable family gift.
- Non-traditional sources (unsecured personal loans, unsecured lines
  of credit) are available only to non-newcomer borrowers with a
  strong credit history at 90.01% to 95% LTV on 1-2 unit purchases.
  NOT available to non-permanent residents.

## Property and program access

- Permanent residents: full access to all homeowner programs (Purchase,
  Self-Employed, Improvement, Home Start, Refinance, etc.) on the same
  terms as Canadian citizens.
- Non-permanent residents: access restricted to purchase and refinance
  loans on 1-4 unit properties with at least one owner-occupied unit.

## Compliance: Prohibition on the Purchase of Residential Property by Non-Canadians Act

The Newcomers program applies only where the borrower is exempt from
the federal Prohibition on the Purchase of Residential Property by
Non-Canadians Act. The assistant should not interpret the Act's
specific exemption categories in chat; that determination is made by
the lender and where needed by legal counsel. The assistant can
acknowledge the Act exists and that it gates non-Canadian buyers'
access to insured purchases, and route specific eligibility questions
to the broker.

## What this overlay file is not

- It is not the base LTV ceiling, property cap, DSR, MQR, credit
  floor, or amortization rules; those are in
  `cmhc-mortgage-loan-insurance.md` and apply identically.
- It is not the full text or interpretation of the Prohibition on the
  Purchase of Residential Property by Non-Canadians Act; eligibility
  under the Act is a legal and lender-side determination.
- It is not a guide to immigration status, visa categories, or
  citizenship applications.
- It is not specific lender overlays on newcomer files; lenders may
  impose stricter requirements than CMHC.
