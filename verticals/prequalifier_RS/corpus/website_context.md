### Alternative Lending FAQ (verbatim Q+A from the page)

What are the typical B lender mortgage rates in Ontario?

> While b lender mortgage rates are slightly higher than
> traditional 'A' banks, they remain significantly more affordable
> than private loans. Rates are typically tiered based on your
> loan-to-value (LTV) and credit profile, offering a cost-effective
> solution for those who don't meet the federal stress test.

Is a B lender mortgage a permanent solution?

> Most clients use an alternative mortgage solution as a strategic
> 1-to-3-year exit strategy. Our goal as your mortgage agent is to
> use this period to stabilize your credit or income documentation
> so you can eventually transition back to lower-rate traditional
> financing.

Who qualifies for alternative lending for small business owners?

> If you are self-employed and your taxable income doesn't reflect
> your actual earnings, you are a prime candidate for a b lender
> mortgage. We use 'stated income' or bank statement programs to
> verify your ability to pay, focusing on your business's success
> rather than just a Notice of Assessment (NOA).

### What is a reverse mortgage

Section heading: "What is a Reverse Mortgage?"

> A reverse mortgage is a financial tool specifically designed for
> Canadian homeowners aged 55 and older. It allows you to convert
> a portion of your home's equity into tax-free cash while you
> maintain 100% ownership and title of your property. Unlike a
> traditional home equity loan, you are not required to make any
> principal or interest payments as long as you live in the home.

### Your trusted reverse mortgage broker

Section heading: "Your Trusted Reverse Mortgage Broker."

> Navigating retirement on a fixed income can be challenging. As
> your reverse mortgage broker, RS Mortgage Solutions helps you
> access up to 55% of your home's value to fund healthcare, home
> renovations, or simply to enjoy a more comfortable lifestyle.

### How does a reverse mortgage work

Section heading: "How Does a Reverse Mortgage Work?"

Stay in Your Home:

> You retain full ownership and are never required to move or
> sell, provided you pay your property taxes and insurance.

No Monthly Payments:

> The interest is added to the balance of the loan, meaning you
> don't have to worry about monthly cash flow.

Tax-Free Funds:

> The money you receive is not considered income, so it won't
> affect your Old Age Security (OAS) or Guaranteed Income
> Supplement (GIS) payments.

Flexible Payouts:

> Choose to receive your money as a single lump sum, planned
> monthly deposits, or a combination of both.

Repayment:

> The loan is only repaid when you choose to move, sell the home,
> or through the estate.
