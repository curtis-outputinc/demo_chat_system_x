# For the practice (advisor-side talking points)

This file is what the assistant speaks to when an advisor or owner is
exploring the demo in advisor-side mode. It is value-talk, not regulatory
copy.

## What the assistant does for a practice

It answers prospective client questions on the practice's website around
the clock, in the practice's own voice, drawing only from what the practice
has put into its knowledge base. It is calibrated to be helpful without
giving specific investment recommendations, return projections, or tax or
legal advice, so it stays inside the lines a licensed advisor needs to
stay inside.

## What the practice gets back

Every conversation feeds a private dashboard the practice owns: how many
visitors chatted, what they asked, what the assistant could not answer (a
direct signal about what to add to the website), how many booked a
consultation or shared their contact, and which pages drive the most
engagement. It turns chat traffic into insight, not just transcripts.

## How it handles new prospect leads

When a visitor signals they are ready to talk to an advisor, the assistant
either books the consultation in chat with the practice's calendar link or
collects the visitor's name and contact in a single brief turn, depending
on which flow the practice prefers. It does not push and does not collect
sensitive financial documents.

## After-hours coverage

The assistant handles late-night retirement planning curiosity, weekend
market nervousness, or the prospect researching options before a Monday
call, so the practice's team is not the only point of contact for every
inbound question.

## Privacy posture

Conversations are kept private and secure and are used only for the
practice's own analysis. The assistant does not forward or share visitor
details outside the practice's own dashboard, and it routes sensitive
documents through a secure approved channel rather than collecting them in
chat.

## What it does not do

The assistant does not give specific investment recommendations, project
returns, recommend specific products without practice-approved guidance,
give tax or legal advice, or make suitability determinations. Practices
testing this should expect it to redirect those questions to the advisor
every time.
