# For the brokerage (broker-side talking points)

This file is what the assistant speaks to when a broker or owner is exploring
the demo in broker-side mode. It is value-talk, not regulatory copy.

## What the assistant does for a brokerage

It answers prospective and current policyholder questions on the
brokerage's website around the clock, in the brokerage's own voice,
drawing only from what the brokerage has put into its knowledge base. It is
calibrated to be helpful without quoting specific premiums, making coverage
decisions, recommending specific carriers, or coaching on claims, so it
stays inside the lines a licensed broker needs to stay inside.

## What the brokerage gets back

Every conversation feeds a private dashboard the brokerage owns: how many
visitors chatted, what they asked, what the assistant could not answer (a
direct signal about what to add to the website), how many booked a
consultation or shared their contact, and which pages drive the most
engagement. It turns chat traffic into insight, not just transcripts.

## How it handles new prospect leads

When a visitor signals they are ready to talk to a broker, the assistant
either books the consultation in chat with the brokerage's calendar link or
collects the visitor's name and contact in a single brief turn, depending
on which flow the brokerage prefers. It does not push and does not collect
sensitive medical or financial documents.

## After-hours coverage

The assistant handles late-night home insurance shopping, weekend
just-got-into-an-accident panic, or the prospect comparing term vs. whole
life at 11 PM, so the brokerage's team is not the only point of contact for
every inbound question.

## Privacy posture

Conversations are kept private and secure and are used only for the
brokerage's own analysis. The assistant does not forward or share visitor
details outside the brokerage's own dashboard, and it routes sensitive
documents through a secure approved channel rather than collecting them in
chat.

## What it does not do

The assistant does not quote premiums, make coverage decisions, recommend
specific carriers without brokerage-approved guidance, give claims advice
on an active claim, give tax or legal advice, or coach a visitor on what to
say to a carrier or adjuster. Brokerages testing this should expect it to
redirect those questions to the broker every time.
