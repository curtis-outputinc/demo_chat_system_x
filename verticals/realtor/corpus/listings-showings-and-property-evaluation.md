# Listings, showings, and property evaluation

**Q: Can you help me find homes for sale?**
Yes. The agent can narrow listings based on budget, location, property type, bedrooms, must-have features, and timing.

**Q: Can you send me listings that match what I am looking for?**
Yes. Share your criteria and contact details and the agent can set up automatic alerts for matching listings.

**Q: Can I schedule a showing through this chat?**
Yes. Share the property address or listing link, your preferred date and time, and your contact details so the agent can confirm.

**Q: How quickly can I see a property?**
That depends on seller availability, tenant notice, lockbox access, and the agent's schedule. Some homes can be shown the same day; others need a day or two of notice.

**Q: Can I view homes over the weekend?**
Yes. Weekends are popular for showings and open houses. Most agents accommodate weekend tours.

**Q: What is the MLS?**
The MLS is a shared listings database real estate professionals use to publish and share property data so listings reach the widest pool of buyers.

**Q: Why do some properties have limited photos online?**
Usually because the home is being prepared, tenants requested privacy, the listing is brand new, or the focus is on land or location rather than the interior.

**Q: Can you find me homes that are not on the market yet?**
The agent may have access to pre-MLS listings, coming-soon properties, or off-market opportunities depending on the area and market.

**Q: What is an unrepresented buyer at an open house?**
A visitor at an open house without their own agent. The agent hosting represents the seller, not the visitor.

**Q: Can I browse homes online if I am not ready to buy yet?**
Of course. Browsing helps you learn the market and refine what you like.

**Q: How often is the website's listing data updated?**
Active listing data syncs with the local MLS regularly. Status changes show up quickly, though brief lag between sources can happen.

**Q: What does "Under Contract" or "Pending" mean?**
The seller has accepted an offer. The deal is moving through inspections, financing, or document review steps before closing.

**Q: Can I view a home that is under contract?**
Sometimes sellers accept backup showings in case the first deal falls through. Priority is given to the buyer currently under contract.

**Q: How do I find out about traffic and noise in the neighbourhood?**
Drive by at different times — rush hour, weekends, and at night — to get a real feel.

**Q: What is "Days on Market" (DOM)?**
DOM is the number of days a property has been listed. A long DOM might signal pricing issues or condition concerns, or it might just mean the right buyer has not arrived yet.

**Q: Is a home bad if it has been on the market a long time?**
Not necessarily. It may be overpriced, hard to show, unusual, or simply waiting for the right buyer. The agent can read the situation.

**Q: What is a walkability score?**
A rating that estimates how easy it is to handle daily errands on foot from a specific address. Useful as one data point, not the whole picture.

**Q: What is a property tax assessment?**
A local government's calculation used to determine property tax. It does not always equal the home's market value.

**Q: What is the difference between a modular home and a manufactured home?**
Modular homes are factory-built in sections and assembled on a traditional foundation under local building codes. Manufactured homes are built on a permanent chassis under federal codes (in the U.S.). They are financed and treated differently.

**Q: Can a home in a historic district be renovated?**
Exterior changes in historic districts usually require approval from a local board to preserve heritage character. Interior work is typically more flexible. Confirm locally.

**Q: What is an easement on a property listing?**
An easement gives someone a legal right to use part of the land for a specific purpose, like utility access or a shared driveway.

**Q: What does "contingent offer accepted" mean on a listing?**
The seller has accepted an offer that still depends on a specific condition, often that the buyer first sells their own home.

**Q: How can I tell if a home has structural or foundation issues?**
Signs include large cracks in walls, doors that do not close, sloping floors, horizontal cracks in basement walls, or water stains. None of these are definitive; an inspector or structural professional should evaluate.

**Q: Do listings mention internet availability?**
Sometimes. The agent can confirm available providers with the seller or check local utility maps.

**Q: What is a multi-family home listing?**
A residential building with multiple separate units (duplex, triplex, fourplex). Owners often live in one unit and rent the others.

**Q: Can you help me find a home with a specific feature, like a pool or a workshop?**
Yes. Share the must-haves and the agent can build a custom shortlist.

**Q: What should I look for during a showing?**
Layout flow, natural light, room size, storage, condition of roof and windows, signs of water issues, smells, noise, and the feel of the neighbourhood. Take notes if you tour multiple homes.

**Q: What questions should I ask about a house I am viewing?**
Age of roof, windows, furnace, AC, electrical panel, and plumbing. Major renovations and whether they were permitted. Insulation, drainage, basement moisture, foundation, and any known repairs.

**Q: What questions should I ask about a condo I am viewing?**
Status certificate or condo documents, reserve fund, condo fees, building rules, recent special assessments, lawsuits, insurance, pet rules, rental rules, parking, locker, and amenities.

**Q: What does "as-is" mean in a listing?**
The seller will not negotiate repairs or credits for known issues. You can still inspect and decide whether the price reflects the condition.

**Q: How do I know if a property is a good investment?**
General signals include local supply and demand, transit and development, schools, comparable rents, and condition. The agent can help analyze a specific property; do not rely on a guarantee of return.

**Q: Can I see a virtual tour?**
Many listings include virtual tours or video walkthroughs. Ask the agent if a tour is available for a specific property.

**Q: Why does the photographer make the home look so different from in person?**
Wide-angle lenses, lighting, and staging often make spaces look larger and brighter than they feel in person. Visit to verify scale and feel.

**Q: Can the agent give honest feedback on a listing I am considering?**
Yes. The agent reviews layout, condition, pricing relative to comparables, building or HOA red flags, and any concerns from prior buyer feedback.
