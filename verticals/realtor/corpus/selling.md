# Selling

**Q: How do I start the process of selling my home?**
Start with a comparative market analysis from the agent to understand the current value. From there, plan preparation (repairs, decluttering, staging), photos, pricing strategy, marketing, showings, offer review, and closing.

**Q: What is my home worth?**
Value depends on recent comparable sales, condition, upgrades, lot, location, market demand, and timing. The agent can prepare a written estimate or arrange a home valuation visit.

**Q: How do you determine my listing price?**
The agent reviews recent comparable sales, active listings, market trends, the property's condition and upgrades, and the seller's timeline. Asking price is a strategy, not a fixed number.

**Q: What is a Comparative Market Analysis?**
A CMA compares your property to recently sold homes, active listings, and expired listings nearby to estimate competitive market value. Sold prices matter more than asking prices.

**Q: Is an online home estimate accurate?**
Online estimates are a starting point but often miss condition, layout, view, finishes, lot, upgrades, and recent local activity. They should not replace a real CMA.

**Q: Should I price my home high to leave room for negotiation?**
Sometimes that works, but overpricing can shrink showings and lengthen days on market. Buyers may also assume something is wrong if a home sits. The right strategy depends on current conditions.

**Q: What happens if my home is overpriced?**
Overpriced homes typically receive fewer showings, fewer offers, and more time on market. Price reductions become harder once a listing has lingered.

**Q: Should I renovate before selling?**
Some upgrades increase value; many do not return their full cost. The agent can advise which fixes and updates make sense for your market and timeline.

**Q: What repairs should I make before selling?**
Focus on visible items: leaks, damaged flooring, broken fixtures, peeling paint, loose handles, dim lighting, and outdated hardware. Big remodels right before listing are usually not worth the spend.

**Q: Should I stage my home?**
Staging can help buyers picture themselves in the space and is especially helpful for vacant homes, unusual layouts, or competitive listings. The agent advises on what is worth staging.

**Q: Do I need professional photos?**
Yes. Most buyers judge a listing online first. Professional photos make a real difference in clicks and showings.

**Q: How should I prepare my home for photography?**
Deep clean, declutter, remove personal photos, open blinds for natural light, tidy the yard, and tuck away small appliances. Less stuff in frame is almost always better.

**Q: How long does it take to sell?**
Timeline depends on price, condition, location, market demand, and competition. Some homes sell quickly; others take weeks or months. The agent gives an honest range during the consultation.

**Q: What is the best time of year to sell?**
Spring and early fall are often active. The best time depends on local demand and your situation; well-presented homes can sell year-round.

**Q: Can I sell my home while living in it?**
Yes. Many sellers stay in the home during the listing period. Keeping it clean and clutter-light and being flexible with showings is what makes it work.

**Q: How should I prepare my home for showings?**
Clean thoroughly, declutter, remove personal items, improve lighting, fix small issues, organize closets, clear counters, and create a welcoming first impression.

**Q: Should I be home during showings?**
Usually no. Buyers feel more comfortable exploring and speaking honestly when the seller is not present.

**Q: How much notice do I need to give for showings?**
That depends on your preferences, the listing terms, and local norms. Many sellers allow same-day showings with reasonable notice; less access usually means fewer showings.

**Q: Can I limit showing times?**
You can, but tighter access usually means fewer buyers can view the home. The agent helps balance convenience and exposure.

**Q: What happens after someone makes an offer on my home?**
The agent reviews the offer with you: price, deposit, conditions, closing date, included items, and buyer strength. You can accept, reject, or counter.

**Q: Do I have to accept the highest offer?**
No. Price matters, but so do deposit, conditions, financing strength, closing date, and the buyer's overall reliability. The strongest offer is not always the highest price.

**Q: What is a counter-offer?**
A counter-offer modifies one or more terms (price, closing date, conditions, items included) and sends the negotiation back to the buyer for review.

**Q: What happens if I accept an offer?**
The buyer works to satisfy any conditions. Once conditions are waived or satisfied, the deal becomes firm and proceeds toward closing.

**Q: What does "sold conditional" mean?**
The seller has accepted an offer, but the deal still depends on certain conditions being met by the buyer or seller.

**Q: What does "sold firm" mean?**
All conditions have been satisfied or waived. The deal is generally moving to closing.

**Q: Can a buyer back out after making an offer?**
That depends on the contract and whether conditions are still in place. If the deal is firm, backing out can have serious legal and financial consequences.

**Q: What if my buyer cannot close?**
The seller may have legal options. The next step is to speak with the agent and a lawyer or settlement professional to review the contract and the situation.

**Q: What costs do sellers pay when selling?**
Common seller costs include real estate commission, legal or settlement fees, mortgage discharge or payoff costs, repairs, staging, moving, and any taxes that apply to your situation.

**Q: How much commission do real estate agents charge?**
Commission varies by market, property type, service level, and agreement. The agent explains the fee structure before listing; specifics are discussed during the consultation.

**Q: Who pays real estate commission?**
Traditionally, the seller pays commission from the sale proceeds at closing, with the structure between brokerages defined in the listing agreement. Practices have shifted in some markets, so the agent confirms what currently applies locally.

**Q: What is included in your commission?**
It typically includes pricing strategy, listing preparation, professional photography, MLS exposure, marketing, showings, negotiation, paperwork support, and transaction management. Exact inclusions depend on the agreement.

**Q: Can I sell my home privately?**
Yes, but selling privately means handling pricing, marketing, inquiries, showings, negotiation, paperwork, and buyer qualification yourself. It is common to net less without representation.

**Q: Why should I use a real estate agent to sell?**
An agent helps price correctly, attract qualified buyers, negotiate stronger terms, manage paperwork and risk, and coordinate the file from listing to closing.

**Q: How will you market my home?**
Marketing usually includes MLS, professional photography, online and social media exposure, signage, email outreach, agent network distribution, and possibly open houses, video, or print, depending on the property and budget.

**Q: Do open houses work?**
Open houses can increase exposure and bring in buyers. Their effectiveness depends on the property, location, and broader marketing strategy.

**Q: Can I sell my home as-is?**
Yes. "As-is" usually means the seller will not negotiate repairs after inspection. Buyers may offer less or build inspection conditions into their offer.

**Q: Should I get a pre-listing inspection?**
A pre-listing inspection can surface issues before the listing goes live and may give buyers more confidence. It is not always necessary, but it can help in some markets.

**Q: Can I sell my home if I still have a mortgage?**
Yes. The mortgage is usually paid off from the sale proceeds at closing, with any payoff or discharge costs deducted.

**Q: What is a listing agreement?**
A listing agreement is a contract authorizing the agent and brokerage to market and handle the sale of your home for a specified timeframe and fee structure.

**Q: Can I reject a full-price offer?**
Yes. Sellers are not required to accept any specific offer, even at the asking price, if the terms do not meet their needs.

**Q: What happens if my home appraises for less than the accepted offer?**
The buyer may need to make up the difference with extra down payment, the price may be renegotiated, or both sides may compromise. The agent and lender walk through options.

**Q: How do we handle multiple offers?**
The agent reviews each offer together with you, looking at price, deposit, financing strength, conditions, closing date, and other terms. Highest price is not always the best total package.

**Q: What is curb appeal and why does it matter?**
Curb appeal is the visual first impression from the street. It sets the tone before buyers walk in.

**Q: What stays with the house when it is sold?**
Generally, anything permanently attached (fixtures, built-ins, landscaping) stays. Appliances and other movable items are negotiable. The contract should list specifics.

**Q: What is the very next step to sell?**
Ask the agent for a current value estimate and a brief listing strategy call. From there, prep and timing become clearer.

**Q: How soon should I contact an agent before selling?**
Several weeks to several months before listing is ideal. Early contact gives time to prepare, time the market, and avoid rushed decisions.

**Q: Can you help me sell and buy at the same time?**
Yes. The agent helps coordinate timelines, pricing, offers, conditions, bridge financing conversations, and moving logistics.

**Q: Should I buy first or sell first?**
That depends on your finances, market conditions, risk tolerance, and housing needs. Selling first reduces risk; buying first secures a specific home but adds complexity. The agent talks through the trade-offs.

**Q: Can I sell my home while it is rented to tenants?**
Yes, but the existing lease and local notice rules must be respected. The agent helps plan showings and timing carefully.

**Q: What is a lockbox?**
A secure device on the property that holds a key. Only authorized agents can open it to show the home.

**Q: How do I cancel a listing agreement if I change my mind about selling?**
Listing agreements have specific cancellation terms. Reach out to the agent to discuss pausing or terminating the listing within the agreed terms.

**Q: What happens on closing day when I sell?**
Ownership transfers, funds are processed, the mortgage and costs are paid, and the remaining proceeds are released to you after closing is complete.
