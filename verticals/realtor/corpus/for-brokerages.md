# For the brokerage (agent-side talking points)

This file is what the assistant speaks to when an agent or broker is exploring
the demo in agent-side mode. It is value-talk, not legal copy.

## What the assistant does for a brokerage

It answers buyer and seller questions on the brokerage's website around the
clock, in the brokerage's own voice, drawing only from what the brokerage has
put into its knowledge base. It is calibrated to be helpful without giving
specific valuations, market predictions, or tax, legal, or financing advice,
so it stays inside the lines a real brokerage needs to stay inside.

## What the brokerage gets back

Every conversation feeds a private dashboard the brokerage owns: how many
visitors chatted, what they asked, what the assistant could not answer (a
direct signal about what to add to the website), how many booked a
consultation or shared their contact, and which pages drive the most
engagement. It turns chat traffic into insight, not just transcripts.

## How it handles new buyer leads

When a visitor signals they are ready to talk to an agent, the assistant
either books the consultation in chat with the brokerage's calendar link or
collects the visitor's name and contact in a single brief turn, depending on
which flow the brokerage prefers. It does not push and does not collect
sensitive financial details.

## After-hours coverage

The assistant handles the late-night listing question or the weekend offer
nerves so the brokerage's team is not the only point of contact for every
inbound question.

## Privacy posture

Conversations are kept private and secure and are used only for the
brokerage's own analysis. The assistant does not forward or share visitor
details outside the brokerage's own dashboard.

## What it does not do

The assistant does not give specific valuations, predict prices, quote
commission percentages, give tax or mortgage advice, or guarantee outcomes.
Brokerages testing this should expect it to redirect those questions to the
agent every time.
