# Mortgages and financing

**Q: Can you recommend a mortgage broker or lender?**
The agent works with trusted mortgage professionals and can make a referral. Buyers should still compare and choose what fits.

**Q: How much can I afford?**
Affordability depends on income, debts, credit, down payment, interest rate, taxes, condo or HOA fees, insurance, and overall monthly comfort. A mortgage professional can give a real number based on the full picture.

**Q: What is a fixed-rate mortgage?**
A mortgage where the interest rate stays the same for a set period. Payments are predictable for that period.

**Q: What is a variable-rate or adjustable-rate mortgage?**
A mortgage where the interest rate can change based on market conditions, which may affect your payment or how much of each payment goes to interest.

**Q: Should I choose fixed or variable financing?**
That depends on your risk tolerance, budget stability, market outlook, and the rate spread. A mortgage professional can compare side by side.

**Q: What is a mortgage stress test?**
A stress test checks whether a borrower can still afford payments if rates rise. Rules vary by country and lender.

**Q: What is mortgage insurance?**
Mortgage insurance may be required when the down payment is below a certain threshold and protects the lender if the borrower defaults. Specifics depend on loan type and location.

**Q: What is a Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratio?**
LTV compares the loan amount to the appraised value of the home. Lenders use it to measure risk before approving a loan.

**Q: What is a Debt-to-Income (DTI) ratio?**
DTI is the percentage of your monthly income that goes to monthly debt payments. Lenders look at it to assess whether a new mortgage is affordable for you.

**Q: What is a mortgage rate lock?**
A guarantee from the lender that holds a specific rate for a set period while your loan is processed. Specifics depend on the lender.

**Q: What is a mortgage point or discount point?**
Points are upfront fees paid to the lender to lower the interest rate. Whether buying points makes sense depends on how long you plan to keep the loan and the rate impact.

**Q: What are lender fees?**
Lender fees are administrative costs charged to process, underwrite, and originate the mortgage. They are usually rolled into closing costs.

**Q: What is a Closing Disclosure?**
A document the lender provides before closing that lists your final loan terms, payment, and closing costs. Specific names and timing vary by jurisdiction.

**Q: Why did my monthly mortgage payment increase?**
Even on a fixed-rate loan, property taxes or insurance may rise, which can change the escrow or tax portion of the payment. Variable-rate loans can change with the underlying rate.

**Q: What is underwriting?**
Underwriting is the lender's review of your income, assets, credit, and the property before final loan approval.

**Q: What documents do I need to apply for a mortgage?**
Common items include identification, recent pay stubs, tax returns, W-2 or T4 slips, bank statements, employment confirmation, and details on the property. The lender provides a specific list.

**Q: Can I make an offer before getting financing approval?**
You can, but it is risky. A financing condition can protect you while your lender finalizes approval; waiving it shifts more risk to you.

**Q: Why does the lender appraise the home?**
The lender appraises to confirm the property is worth enough to support the loan amount.

**Q: What happens if the appraisal is lower than the purchase price?**
You may need to renegotiate the price, increase your down payment, find another lender, or reconsider the deal. Options depend on the contract and lender.

**Q: What is a bridge loan?**
A short-term loan that lets a homeowner borrow against equity in their current home to fund a down payment on a new home before the current one sells.

**Q: What is a HELOC?**
A revolving line of credit secured against home equity. Common uses include renovations or consolidating higher-interest debt. Terms vary by lender.

**Q: What is cash-out refinancing?**
Replacing your current mortgage with a new, larger one and taking the difference in cash. Whether it makes sense depends on the rate and your goals.

**Q: Can I buy a home with student loans or other debt?**
Often yes. Lenders look at overall debt-to-income, credit, and your ability to handle the new payment.

**Q: What is a conventional loan?**
A standard mortgage not backed by a government program. Guidelines and down payment requirements vary.

**Q: What is an FHA loan?**
A mortgage insured by the U.S. Federal Housing Administration, designed to help buyers with lower down payments or credit profiles. Eligibility and rules apply.

**Q: What is a VA loan?**
A U.S. mortgage option backed by the Department of Veterans Affairs, available to qualifying military members, veterans, and certain surviving spouses.

**Q: What is a USDA loan?**
A U.S. mortgage program for designated rural areas, with eligibility tied to income and location.

**Q: What is a jumbo loan?**
A mortgage that exceeds the conforming loan limit set by federal housing finance agencies. Used to finance higher-priced properties.

**Q: What minimum credit score do I need to buy a house?**
That depends on the loan program and lender. Higher scores generally unlock better rates. A mortgage professional can review your profile.

**Q: Final approval is not the same as pre-approval, right?**
Right. Pre-approval estimates what you can borrow based on the documents you provided. Final approval depends on the property, appraisal, and full underwriting.

**Q: Should I pay off debt before applying?**
Reducing high-interest debt can improve your application and the rate you qualify for. The right move depends on your savings, your timeline, and the lender's rules.

**Q: Can I use gifted money for a down payment?**
Often yes. Lenders usually require a signed gift letter and proof the funds were transferred. Rules differ by program.

**Q: I am self-employed. Can I get a mortgage?**
Yes. You usually need two years of tax returns, business income records, and possibly bank statements to verify stable income.

**Q: What is an escrow or tax account on a mortgage?**
An account where a portion of each monthly payment is held to cover property taxes and insurance. The lender pays those bills from this account when due.

**Q: What is an amortization schedule?**
A schedule showing how each monthly payment splits between interest and principal over the life of the loan.

**Q: Should I quote me an interest rate right now?**
Rates change daily and depend on your profile. A licensed mortgage professional can quote you accurately. The agent can refer you.

**Q: I am an out-of-country buyer. Can I get a mortgage here?**
It depends on your residency, income source, and the lender. Cross-border buyers usually work with lenders who specialize in non-resident financing. There may also be additional taxes; check with a local tax professional.

**Q: What is the difference between principal and interest?**
Principal is the amount you borrowed. Interest is the cost of borrowing it. Each monthly payment includes both.
