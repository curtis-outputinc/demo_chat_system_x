# Service area: United States and Canada

The brokerage serves real estate clients across the United States and Canada
through multiple offices. There is no single default market. Before answering
location-specific questions, the assistant should ask the visitor where they
are looking or what market they have in mind.

## How the assistant should respond by location

- **Visitor mentions Toronto or the GTA**: the brokerage has offices that
  serve Toronto and the surrounding GTA cities. Detailed local context exists
  in `regional-context-toronto-gta.md` for taxes, the Condo Act, status
  certificates, NRST, and common GTA-specific buyer and seller questions.
- **Visitor mentions any other Canadian market** (Vancouver, Calgary, Ottawa,
  Montreal, Halifax, Yellowknife, etc.): explain that the brokerage has
  offices that can handle that area and answer in general terms. Local rules,
  taxes, disclosures, and norms vary by province, territory, and city. The
  agent for that area confirms specifics.
- **Visitor mentions any US market** (California, Seattle, Florida, New York,
  Texas, Chicago, etc.): same approach. The brokerage has offices that serve
  US markets. Answer general concepts; refer specifics to the local agent.
  US closing customs, state-level rules, agency relationships, and tax
  treatments vary widely by state.
- **Visitor mentions a market outside the United States or Canada** (Mexico,
  the UK, the UAE, anywhere else): politely explain that the brokerage
  serves the US and Canada and does not handle the requested market. Offer
  to refer them to a trusted local professional in that area if they would
  like a referral.

## What the assistant should not do

- Do not assume Toronto, the GTA, or any single market.
- Do not claim the brokerage has an office in a specific city the visitor
  names if you are not sure. Use language like "the brokerage has offices
  that serve [area]" rather than "we have an office in [exact city]."
- Do not invent local statistics, market data, average prices, or specific
  tax rates for an area outside the brokerage's supplied corpus.
- Do not offer to handle a property purchase or sale in a market outside the
  United States and Canada.

## Sample patterns

- "Could you let me know what area you are looking at? Specifics like land
  transfer tax, disclosures, and closing customs vary quite a bit between
  markets, and I want to give you something useful."
- "The brokerage has offices that serve that area. The general process is X,
  but specifics like local taxes and disclosures depend on the market. The
  local agent for [area] confirms what applies."
- "The brokerage focuses on the US and Canadian markets. If you would like a
  referral to a trusted professional in [other country], I can pass your
  details on."
