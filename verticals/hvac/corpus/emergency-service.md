# 24/7 emergency service

ABC HVAC's emergency line answers 24 hours a day, 7 days a week
including holidays. Call **(123) 555-5555** any time.

## What counts as an emergency

Call the emergency line if any of the following:

- **Gas smell** anywhere in the home or building (natural gas,
  propane, rotten-egg / sulfur smell). Get out first, call 911 or
  the gas utility from outside, then call us to dispatch a tech.
- **Carbon monoxide alarm going off**, or symptoms of CO exposure
  (headache, nausea, dizziness in multiple people or pets near
  the furnace or a gas appliance). Get out into fresh air, call
  911, then call us.
- **Smoke or burning smell** from any HVAC equipment, or visible
  flame outside the burner assembly.
- **No heat in freezing weather**, especially with vulnerable
  occupants (elderly, infants, medical equipment, or pipes at
  risk of freezing).
- **No cooling in extreme heat**, especially with vulnerable
  occupants.
- **Water pouring** from a furnace, boiler, AC air handler, or
  rooftop unit in a way that is damaging the building.
- **Refrigerant smell** (sweet, chemical, sometimes called
  chloroform-like) with active AC operation and cooling loss.
- **Commercial buildings**: any HVAC failure that is stopping
  operations, threatening tenants, or endangering inventory
  (server room over-temp, restaurant walk-in with a shared
  ventilation issue, etc.).

## What to do while waiting for the tech

Depending on what is happening:

- **Gas smell**: leave the building now. Do not flip any switches
  or use your phone inside the building. Call 911 or your gas
  utility from outside. Do not go back in for belongings.
- **Carbon monoxide alarm**: get everyone outside into fresh air,
  leave the door open, call 911. Do not go back in.
- **Smoke or burning from equipment**: shut it off at the switch
  or the breaker if you can do it safely, and step away.
- **Active flood from HVAC equipment**: shut off the equipment at
  the breaker if it is safe to reach. If the leak is a boiler or
  hydronic line, shut off the water feed if you can find it.
- **No heat in freezing weather**: keep taps dripping and open
  cabinet doors on exterior walls to protect pipes. Do you have
  somewhere warm you can be while we get a tech out?
- **No cooling in extreme heat**: close blinds on sun-facing
  windows, drink water, and check on anyone vulnerable.
- **Sparking outlet or panel near HVAC equipment**: kill the
  breaker for that circuit if you can safely reach it.

## What the emergency call looks like

- You call (123) 555-5555.
- Our dispatcher answers and gets your name, address, phone
  number, and a short description of what is happening.
- We prioritize based on safety. Gas smells, CO alarms, no heat
  in extreme cold with vulnerable occupants, and structural
  water damage go first.
- We give you an ETA. Most emergency dispatches happen within a
  few hours; extreme weather events (a cold snap or a heat wave)
  can stretch that as many calls come in at once.
- Emergency service is billed at our after-hours rate (see
  `pricing-and-financing.md`). Platinum residential plans and
  Monthly commercial plans have this waived.

## What is NOT an emergency

Some situations feel urgent but are not safety issues. We still
service these, just on the regular schedule:

- Filter needs changing.
- Thermostat is showing a "change battery" warning.
- One room is warmer or colder than others.
- Furnace is running fine but the utility bill is higher than
  usual.
- Annual maintenance is overdue.
- Buying a new system or getting a replacement estimate.

Call the main line during business hours for these.

## Emergency service on maintenance plans

Platinum residential customers and Monthly commercial customers
have the after-hours rate waived on emergency dispatches. Other
plan tiers get priority scheduling and discounted repair rates but
still pay the after-hours rate if a tech has to be dispatched
outside regular business hours.
