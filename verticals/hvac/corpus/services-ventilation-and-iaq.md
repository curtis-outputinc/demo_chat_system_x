# Ventilation and indoor air quality

Cooling and heating are only part of what an HVAC system does. The
other half is moving the right amount of the right kind of air
through your home or building.

## Ductwork

We design, install, repair, and seal residential and light
commercial ductwork:

- New construction and remodel duct design (Manual D for right-
  sized duct sizing).
- Sealing leaky joints with mastic or aeroseal so the conditioned
  air actually reaches the rooms it is supposed to.
- Rebalancing airflow so no room is always too hot or too cold.
- Repairing crushed, disconnected, or damaged flex duct.
- Cleaning heavily contaminated ductwork after renovations,
  rodent problems, or mold events.
- Adding zoning with motorized dampers and multiple thermostats
  so different areas of the home can hold different temperatures.

Duct cleaning is not something every home needs on a schedule.
We recommend it every 3 to 5 years if there are no red flags, or
sooner if you see visible dust falling out of vents, had a major
renovation, had rodents in the system, or notice a persistent
smell that filter changes will not clear.

## Whole-home humidifiers

Winter air in a heated home is often dry (comfort range is 30% to
50% relative humidity, and heated air routinely drops below that).
We install bypass, fan-powered, and steam humidifiers that connect
to the furnace and add moisture to the airstream. Signs a
humidifier would help: dry skin, static shocks, nose or throat
dryness, cracking wood floors or furniture.

## Whole-home dehumidifiers

Muggy summers or damp basements can push humidity above 60%, which
feels sticky and encourages mold. Whole-home dehumidifiers tie into
the ductwork and pull moisture out until the humidity holds steady
at your target.

## HRVs and ERVs

Heat Recovery Ventilators (HRV) and Energy Recovery Ventilators
(ERV) bring fresh outdoor air into a tightly built home while
exhausting stale indoor air, without wasting the energy already
spent conditioning that air. ERVs also transfer moisture between
the two streams, which helps humidity control. Great for newer,
tighter homes where stuffiness or moisture is a problem.

## Air filters

Standard 1-inch pleated filters change every 1 to 3 months. Homes
with pets, heavy dust, or allergy sufferers should check the filter
monthly. Higher-MERV filters (MERV 11, 13, 16) trap smaller
particles but restrict airflow more, so they only belong on systems
whose blowers are strong enough to push through them. We check
static pressure before installing high-MERV filters.

MERV rating quick reference:

- MERV 6 to 8: basic pleated, catches dust and pollen.
- MERV 8 to 11: solid residential choice for most homes.
- MERV 13: captures viruses, smoke particles, fine allergens.
  Standard for commercial spaces.
- MERV 16 and up (HEPA in supported systems): for allergy or
  respiratory sensitivity.

## Air purifiers

Whole-home purifiers install inside the ductwork:

- UV-C germicidal lights kill mold spores, bacteria, and viruses
  circulating through the coil and duct.
- Electronic and ionization purifiers charge particles so they
  drop out of the airstream.
- Media air cleaners are essentially very deep, very high-MERV
  filter cabinets that install inline.

The right choice depends on what you are trying to control:
allergens, odors, biological growth, or all three.

## Kitchen and bath exhaust

We install, replace, and troubleshoot range hoods, downdraft
vents, over-the-range microwave exhaust, and bathroom exhaust
fans. For commercial kitchens, we handle the hood-plus-make-up-air
combo (see `services-commercial.md`).

## Air balancing

If some rooms are always too hot or too cold, or the return
airflow does not match the supply, we can balance the system by
adjusting dampers, sizing registers correctly, sealing leaks, and
right-sizing return grilles. Comfort improves without changing
equipment.
