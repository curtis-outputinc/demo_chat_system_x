# About ABC Comfort Heating and Air Conditioning

ABC Comfort Heating and Air Conditioning is a full-service heating, cooling, and ventilation company
serving homeowners and businesses. We have been in business for over
15 years and are a fully licensed, bonded, and insured Texas HVAC
contractor. Our technicians are trained on furnaces, air
conditioners, heat pumps, ductless mini-splits, boilers, rooftop
units, chillers, and commercial ventilation systems.

## What we do

We handle everything HVAC across residential and commercial:

- **Heating**: gas furnaces, electric furnaces, boilers, heat
  pumps, ductless heat pumps, radiant heat, in-floor heat, unit
  heaters for shops and warehouses.
- **Cooling**: central air conditioning, ductless mini-splits,
  heat pumps in cooling mode, window and portable unit service.
- **Ventilation and indoor air quality**: ductwork design, repair,
  and sealing; HRVs and ERVs; whole-home humidifiers; whole-home
  dehumidifiers; high-efficiency filters; UV air purifiers.
- **Controls**: thermostats, smart thermostats, zoning systems,
  building automation integration for commercial clients.
- **Maintenance**: annual and biannual tune-ups, seasonal
  inspections, preventive maintenance plans.
- **Commercial**: rooftop units (RTUs), split systems, packaged
  units, chillers and cooling towers, boilers, VRF systems,
  make-up air units, kitchen exhaust integration, and building
  automation.
- **24/7 emergency service** for no-heat in cold weather, no-AC
  in extreme heat, gas smells, refrigerant leaks, and water
  damage from HVAC equipment.

## Who we serve

Homeowners, landlords, property managers, and commercial clients
including offices, retail, restaurants, warehouses, medical
clinics, schools, apartment buildings, and industrial facilities.
We work with new construction, remodels, tenant improvements, and
straight equipment replacements.

## What we do not do

- Plumbing repairs not related to HVAC equipment (a leaky faucet,
  a running toilet, a burst supply line): we refer to a plumber.
- Electrical work not tied to HVAC installations (a broken outlet,
  a light fixture, a panel upgrade): we refer to an electrician.
- Appliance repair (dishwasher, dryer, oven): we do not service
  household appliances.
- Structural work, roofing, or general remodeling.

If a caller has a question in one of these areas, we point them
to the right kind of contractor and offer to look at any HVAC
side of the job.
