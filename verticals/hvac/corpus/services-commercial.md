# Commercial HVAC services

ABC HVAC serves commercial and light industrial clients: offices,
retail, restaurants, warehouses, medical clinics, apartment
buildings, schools, gyms, and manufacturing facilities. We handle
new equipment installs, repairs, replacements, and ongoing
preventive maintenance under service agreements.

## Rooftop units (RTUs)

Packaged rooftop units are the workhorse of commercial HVAC:
compressor, evaporator, condenser, and heating stage all in one
weatherized cabinet, mounted on the roof, ducted straight down into
the building. We service every major manufacturer, both gas and
electric heat models. Common RTU calls: no cooling on a hot day,
no heat in winter, water leaking through the roof / ceiling below,
economizer stuck open or closed, condenser fan motor failure,
compressor lockout.

Rooftop replacements typically involve a mobile crane, a
structural review to confirm the roof can handle the new unit,
curb adapter fabrication, electrical and gas line adjustments,
control retie-in, and coordination around business hours.

## Split systems and packaged units

Traditional split systems (separate condenser and air handler),
small packaged units at ground level, and packaged terminal air
conditioners (PTAC) for hotels and multifamily.

## VRF and VRV systems

Variable Refrigerant Flow systems: one outdoor condenser drives
many indoor units, each modulating independently. Uniquely
efficient for offices, hotels, and multi-tenant spaces because it
can heat some rooms while cooling others simultaneously.

## Chillers and boilers

Air-cooled and water-cooled chillers feeding air handlers via
chilled-water piping loops. Cooling towers on the reject side.
Commercial boilers on the heating side, including condensing
gas-fired and hydronic loops for radiators or in-floor heating in
large buildings.

## Make-up air units (MAUs)

Restaurants and commercial kitchens run massive exhaust hoods
that pull huge volumes of air out of the space. A make-up air
unit feeds tempered outside air back in so the kitchen does not
end up in negative pressure. Without a working MAU, kitchen doors
get impossible to open, backdrafting is a risk, and the exhaust
loses its effectiveness. We install, service, and rebalance MAUs.

## Dedicated outdoor air systems (DOAS)

Ventilation-only systems that pre-condition fresh outdoor air
before it enters the building's main HVAC loops. Common in
buildings pursuing high indoor air quality or LEED targets.

## Server room and IT cooling

Precision cooling (CRAC) units that run 24/7 with tight temperature
and humidity control to protect servers. We install and service
CRAC and CRAH units, and we can retrofit standard cooling into
dedicated IT-room coverage where needed.

## Controls and building automation

Programmable thermostats, direct digital controls (DDC), and
integration with building automation systems (BAS) so a facility
manager can set schedules, monitor equipment health, get remote
alerts, and shed load during utility peak-demand events. We work
with existing BAS platforms and coordinate with controls specialists
on larger projects.

## Preventive maintenance for commercial

Commercial systems benefit from more frequent service than
residential. Standard tiers we offer:

- Quarterly (four visits a year): standard for offices, retail,
  and light commercial.
- Bi-monthly (six visits a year): for restaurants, medical, and
  buildings with heavy usage.
- Monthly (twelve visits a year): for buildings with critical
  operations, high occupancy, or specialty equipment.

Every visit includes filter changes, coil inspection, belt tension
and wear check, refrigerant and pressure check, electrical
inspection (voltage, amp draws, contactor condition), drain and pan
cleaning, safety control testing, and a written service report
with any recommended repairs.

## Ventilation and code compliance

We help commercial clients meet ventilation rate requirements
(ASHRAE 62.1) for their space and occupancy, plus air pressure
balancing so doors work, odors do not travel between spaces, and
buildings do not backdraft.

## What we handle for restaurants specifically

- Exhaust hood commissioning and rebalancing.
- Make-up air unit service.
- Grease-laden air handling and code-compliant filtration.
- Dining room comfort in a high-heat kitchen environment.
- Refrigeration split from HVAC: we handle the air side; we refer
  walk-in cooler and freezer refrigeration to a specialty
  contractor.

## What we do about permits

New commercial installs, replacements above a size threshold, and
work that touches gas piping or high-voltage electrical generally
require a permit. We pull the permit and coordinate the inspection
as part of the job. On tenant improvement work we coordinate with
the general contractor and building engineer.
