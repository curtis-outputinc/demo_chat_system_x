# For HVAC shop owners (professional side)

This vertical is a working demonstration of an Intelligent Website
Chat System designed for HVAC contractors: shops that run heating,
cooling, ventilation, or all three. When a business owner is
evaluating this tool for their own website, this file gives the
context.

## What the system does for an HVAC business

- **Catches inbound around the clock.** Emergency calls happen at
  2 AM when a furnace fails during a cold snap, or on a Saturday
  when an AC dies during a heatwave. The office is not answering.
  This system does.
- **Screens the conversation.** Before a truck rolls, the system
  captures the problem, urgency, address, and phone. That means
  fewer wasted dispatches to jobs that turn out to be a dead
  thermostat battery.
- **Books service calls inline.** The chat routes to the shop's
  existing booking system (Cal.com, ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro,
  whatever the shop uses) or captures a callback in the
  meantime.
- **Flags emergencies fast.** Gas smells, CO alarms, no-heat in
  freezing weather, no-AC in extreme heat get the emergency-
  response pattern in the reply and are surfaced immediately in
  the handoff to the dispatcher.
- **Stays in the shop's voice.** Tone, branding, service area,
  hours, pricing posture, and specific services are configured
  per shop. Nothing is generic.
- **Filters out non-work.** People asking for parts pricing,
  wanting to sell equipment, asking for career info, spam - the
  system handles or redirects without eating dispatcher time.

## What the shop gets in the handoff

Every completed conversation produces a clean handoff to the
office:

- Customer name and phone
- Address (city and general area during chat; exact address on
  the callback)
- Equipment type (AC, furnace, boiler, heat pump, ductless, RTU,
  etc.)
- Symptom in the customer's words
- Urgency flag (emergency, time-sensitive, or standard schedule)
- Any safety flag (gas smell, CO alarm, smoke)
- Best time of day for a callback

This means the dispatcher walks into every call with context, not
cold.

## What the shop does not have to do

- Answer the phone at 11 PM.
- Field pricing questions that never turn into work.
- Return five voicemails to book one appointment.
- Explain what a HRV or a heat pump is a dozen times a week.
- Chase leads that showed interest at 8 PM Sunday and are gone
  by Monday morning.

## What it does not replace

- Truck rolls: an on-site diagnosis still needs a tech.
- Estimate visits: replacement quotes still need a comfort
  advisor in person.
- Bookkeeping and dispatch software.
- Skilled technicians. The system routes to them; it does not
  replace them.

## Pricing model

The system runs as a monthly subscription. Setup takes about a
week: initial intake call, corpus configuration, tone review, chat
placement on the shop's website, test conversations, go-live.
After launch, the shop pays a flat monthly fee that includes
hosting, model costs, and standard configuration updates.

Larger shops (multi-location, high call volume, custom booking
integrations) get a custom quote that reflects the scale.

## Common questions from shop owners

**Q: Does this replace my dispatcher?**
No. It filters and captures work before the dispatcher gets
involved. The dispatcher still books the calendar and manages the
techs. This just means the dispatcher spends time on real work,
not on tire-kickers.

**Q: Can it book directly into ServiceTitan / Housecall Pro / other software?**
For most systems yes, through an integration or a booking link.
The exact setup depends on which software the shop uses.

**Q: What happens if the AI gives a wrong answer?**
The corpus is where every fact lives. If a fact is wrong or
missing, we update the corpus and it fixes the answer everywhere.
The system also has hard rules that prevent inventing prices,
promising specific arrival times, or diagnosing over chat.

**Q: How does it handle emergencies?**
Emergency detection is the highest-priority pattern in the
behavior file. Gas smell, CO alarm, no-heat / no-AC in extreme
weather, smoke, refrigerant leaks all trigger the emergency
response: safety-first instructions, priority handoff, phone
capture, no full intake bureaucracy.

**Q: Can it screen out tire-kickers?**
It cannot force someone to be a good lead, but the funnel is
designed to filter naturally. People asking for a specific price
get the honest answer (we quote after seeing the equipment), which
weeds out those who only want a number. People who want service
get through to booking cleanly.

**Q: How is my customer data handled?**
Every shop's data is isolated. Conversations, leads, and contact
info stay with the shop's own database. See the privacy file for
the specifics.

**Q: What does setup look like?**
An initial call to understand the shop: services, service area,
hours, emergency policy, pricing posture, tone, staffing model.
Then we build the corpus (usually 5 to 10 pages of shop-specific
content), configure the tone, and set up the chat placement on
the shop's website. First conversations happen in the first week.

**Q: How do I see what people are asking?**
The shop gets access to a dashboard with every conversation,
transcript, lead capture, and top-questions report. Emergencies
are flagged. Unanswered questions are surfaced so the corpus can
be improved over time.

**Q: What if I want to try it before signing up?**
That is what this demo is for. Chat with it. Ask hard questions.
See how it handles emergencies, pricing pressure, and
out-of-scope requests. When ready, book a call.
