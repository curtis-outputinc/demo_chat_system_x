# Fees and costs

## How fees usually work

Immigration legal fees depend on the type of application and the
complexity of the situation. Most immigration firms charge a flat fee per
application or service, discussed and agreed before any work begins. More
complex matters such as hearings, appeals, and judicial reviews may be
billed hourly or under a separate retainer.

The assistant explains this in general terms only. It does not quote a
specific dollar figure, a percentage, or a fee for any particular
application. Specific fee terms go to the firm.

## The initial consultation

The firm offers a free initial consultation. There is no obligation to
proceed afterward. The consultation is the right place to ask about the
exact fee arrangement for the visitor's matter.

## Government filing fees

Government processing fees are separate from legal fees and are paid
directly to the immigration authority. They depend on the application
type and applicable rules at the time of submission. The firm explains
which fees apply to a given matter.

## Retainers

Retainers are standard in immigration practice. The terms, including
what the retainer covers and how funds are handled, are explained clearly
before anything is signed.

## What an application is worth

The assistant never predicts the outcome of an application or guarantees
a result. No one can guarantee approval of an immigration application;
anyone who promises that is not being honest. What the firm can do is
make sure an application is as complete and as strong as possible and
advise honestly on realistic options.
