# CMHC Self-Employed program (Federal Canada)

Overlay on the CMHC homeownership base. Use this file only for the
self-employed-specific rules and documentation; everything else (LTV
ceilings, down payment tiers, DSR limits, MQR, credit floor,
amortization rules, eligibility, property location, premium framework)
comes from `cmhc-mortgage-loan-insurance.md`.

The Self-Employed program is offered at NO additional premium cost
compared to the standard Purchase program. The base premium schedule
applies.

## Who qualifies as self-employed

- Sole proprietorships.
- Partnerships.
- Incorporated companies.

A minimum of 24 months operating the business OR 24 months of
equivalent experience in the same line of work is recommended.

Additional flexibilities apply for borrowers who have been operating
their business for less than 24 months or have been in the same line
of work for less than 24 months. Eligibility for the shorter-history
case is confirmed at the lender level and depends on factors like
business acquisition history, cash reserves, and a demonstrated
history of credit management.

## Income verification

Two primary documentation paths:

- T1 General accompanied by the Notice of Assessment (NOA).
- Proof of Income statement (POI).

Either is sufficient for the standard income verification.

## Income grossing-up

Recognizing that self-employed borrowers typically deduct business
expenses, income from self-employment in the case of sole
proprietorships or partnerships may be increased for qualification
purposes in one of two ways:

- 15% gross-up. Add 15% to the declared self-employment income. This
  is the simpler path.
- "Add-back" approach. Add back specific eligible deductions to the
  declared income.

## Eligible deductions for the add-back approach

Eligible deductions are LIMITED to:

- Business-use-of-home expenses.
- Motor-vehicle expenses.
- Capital cost allowances (CCA).

Other deductions cannot be added back under this program.

## Verification documents for the add-back approach

When using the add-back approach, self-employment income may be
verified through any of:

- Audited financial statements.
- Financial statements accompanied by a Review Engagement Report
  signed by a practicing accountant.
- Statement of Business or Professional Activities (T2125)
  accompanied by the NOA.

## Other supporting documentation that may be requested

Depending on the borrower's circumstances, additional documentation
may include:

- Income tax returns with NOA.
- Business credit reports.
- GST returns.
- Active business account statements.
- Business license or articles of incorporation.
- Previous employment records (relevant for recently self-employed
  borrowers transitioning from employment).
- Signed contracts.
- Recent account statements.

## What this overlay file is not

- It is not the base LTV ceiling, property cap, DSR, MQR, credit
  floor, or amortization rules; those are in
  `cmhc-mortgage-loan-insurance.md` and apply identically.
- It is not a description of the Small Rental Loan product, which is
  separately available through the Self-Employed program at up to 80%
  LTV, 2-4 units, $1M property cap, 20% minimum down, 25-year
  amortization.
- It is not specific lender overlays on self-employed files; lenders
  may impose stricter documentation than CMHC requires.
