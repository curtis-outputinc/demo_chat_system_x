# Private mortgage default insurers in Canada (Sagen, Canada Guaranty)

CMHC is one of three mortgage default insurers operating in Canada.
The other two are private insurers: Sagen Canada and Canada Guaranty.
Both operate on a framework parallel to CMHC's, with their own
underwriting policies and premium schedules. OSFI's B-20 requires
lenders to meet the chosen insurer's requirements to keep insurance
valid (see `osfi-b20-residential-mortgage-underwriting.md`).

Practical implication: a borrower's lender chooses which insurer to
work with based on file fit and lender-insurer relationships. The
borrower typically does not pick the insurer directly. CMHC, Sagen,
and Canada Guaranty all backstop high-ratio insured mortgages with
similar product structures.

## Source URLs

- Sagen Canada: https://www.sagen.ca/
- Canada Guaranty: https://www.canadaguaranty.ca/

## Sagen Canada

### Named programs (captured from Sagen's homepage)

- Homebuyer 95 Program: high-ratio purchase with as little as 5%
  down. Parallel to CMHC's Purchase program.
- New to Canada Program: newcomers with as little as 5% down.
  Parallel to CMHC Newcomers.
- Business for Self (Alt. A): self-employed program. Parallel to
  CMHC Self-Employed.
- Homeowner Assistance Program (HOAP): support program for
  homeowners experiencing financial hardship, aimed at preventing
  foreclosure.

### 30-year amortization

- Sagen has indicated a recent policy expansion supporting 30-year
  amortization. Specific eligibility for the 30-year option (LTV
  > 80%, first-time-buyer or new build, matching the CMHC Home Start
  rule) parallels CMHC.

### What was NOT captured from the source

- Specific LTV maximums.
- Specific premium rate tables.
- Specific underwriting criteria distinguishing Sagen from CMHC.
- The Sagen homepage refers to "Underwriting Policies & Standards
  (UPS)" but those are housed in professional-lender resources, not
  the public homepage.

## Canada Guaranty

### Product lineup (captured from Canada Guaranty's homepage)

Canada Guaranty offers nine homeowner mortgage insurance products:

1. Downpayment Advantage.
2. Flex 95 Advantage (borrowed down payment, parallel to CMHC's
   non-traditional down payment program at 90.01-95% LTV).
3. Lifestyle Advantage (second home).
4. Low Doc Advantage (self-employed, parallel to CMHC Self-Employed).
5. Maple Leaf Advantage (newcomers to Canada, parallel to CMHC
   Newcomers).
6. Progress Draw Advantage (parallel to CMHC's progress-draw
   advancing option for construction or improvement files).
7. Purchase Advantage Plus.
8. Refinance to Add Secondary Suites (parallel to CMHC's secondary
   suite refinance program).
9. Rental Advantage (parallel to CMHC's Small Rental Loans).

### Specialized programs

- Energy-Efficient Advantage: parallel to CMHC's Eco premium refunds.
- Portable Advantage: parallel to CMHC's Portability feature.
- Homeownership Solutions Program: hardship workout solutions for
  borrowers facing financial difficulty, parallel to Sagen's HOAP.

### What distinguishes Canada Guaranty

- 100% Canadian-owned private mortgage insurer.
- Established 2010.
- Positions itself on flexibility and personalized lender support.

### What was NOT captured from the source

- Specific down payment minimums per product.
- Specific LTV ceilings per product.
- Specific underwriting criteria per product.
- These details are restricted to professional-lender resources
  documents, not the public homepage.

## Practical framing for the agent

- The agent does NOT pick a specific insurer for a borrower. The
  lender does that as part of underwriting.
- The agent CAN acknowledge that Sagen and Canada Guaranty are
  alternatives to CMHC, with parallel product structures.
- If a borrower asks "which insurer is best for me?" — the answer is
  the broker decides based on lender relationships and file fit.
- The CMHC corpus files (base, self-employed overlay, newcomers
  overlay, GDS / TDS calculation) describe the framework for ALL
  three insurers at a structural level. Where Sagen or Canada
  Guaranty has a quirk that diverges (e.g. Sagen's HOAP hardship
  program), the agent can mention it as a possibility and route
  specifics to the broker.

## What this corpus file is not

- It is not a per-product underwriting matrix for Sagen or Canada
  Guaranty.
- It is not the current premium rate schedules for either private
  insurer.
- It is not a guide for which insurer a borrower should request;
  borrowers typically don't pick.
- It is not a recommendation to use any specific insurer.
