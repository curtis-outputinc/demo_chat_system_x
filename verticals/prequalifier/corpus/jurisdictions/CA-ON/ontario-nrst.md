# Ontario Non-Resident Speculation Tax (NRST)

Provincial tax applied to qualifying Ontario residential property
purchases by foreign entities. Sits ON TOP of the regular Ontario
land transfer tax.

## Source URLs

- NRST overview: https://www.ontario.ca/document/non-resident-speculation-tax
- NRST rebates and refunds: https://www.ontario.ca/document/non-resident-speculation-tax/non-resident-speculation-tax-rebates-and-refunds

## Current rate and effective date

- 25% of the value of the consideration.
- Rate became 25% effective October 25, 2022.
- Prior to that date the rate was lower (the current canonical page
  documents 25% as the current rate; historical rates live with the
  Ministry of Finance).

## Who pays the NRST

The NRST applies to "foreign entities" and "taxable trustees":

- Foreign nationals: individuals who are not Canadian citizens AND not
  permanent residents of Canada.
- Foreign corporations: corporations not incorporated in Canada, OR
  Canadian-incorporated corporations controlled by foreign entities.
- Taxable trustees: trustees of trusts with foreign-entity
  beneficiaries or foreign-entity trustees.

If ANY ONE transferee is a foreign entity, the NRST applies to 100% of
the transaction value. It is NOT prorated to the foreign party's
ownership percentage.

## What properties are subject to NRST

"Designated land" containing 1-6 single-family residences:

- Detached, semi-detached, townhouse.
- Residential condominium.
- Cottage or cabin.
- Duplex through sixplex.
- As of March 27, 2024: parking and storage units in condominium
  complexes are also covered.

Multi-unit apartment buildings with 7+ units are exempt.

## Geographic scope

The NRST applies province-wide across Ontario. Not limited to a
specific region.

## Interaction with the regular LTT

- The NRST is in ADDITION to the regular Ontario Land Transfer Tax.
- It does NOT replace or sit on top of LTT in a compounding way; both
  apply separately to the same transaction.
- See `ontario-land-transfer-tax.md` for the LTT framework.

## Rebates available

### Permanent Resident Rebate (full NRST refund)

- A foreign national who becomes a Canadian permanent resident may
  receive a FULL NRST refund.
- The applicant must become a permanent resident within four years
  from the date of purchase or acquisition.
- The applicant must occupy the property as their principal residence.
- Application deadline: within 180 days of becoming a permanent
  resident.
- Proof of occupancy must be provided through documents such as
  updated driver's license, credit card statement, or bank statement.
  Utility bills, property tax bills, and home insurance are NOT
  accepted as occupancy proof.

### Industrial Use Rebate

- Available when the property is reclassified into an industrial,
  large industrial, or aggregate extraction class within six years of
  purchase.
- Rebate amount depends on the proportion of land receiving
  industrial classification.
- Application deadline: within six years of conveyance registration.

### General refunds

- Submit within four years of NRST payment.

## Discontinued rebates

Two transitional rebates expired March 31, 2025 and are NO LONGER
available:

- International student NRST rebate.
- Foreign national working in Ontario NRST rebate.

Both applied only to purchases under agreements dated on or before
March 29, 2022. Newer purchases never had access to these.

## How to apply

- Online through the Ministry of Finance portal.
- Or by mailing completed affidavits with supporting documents.
