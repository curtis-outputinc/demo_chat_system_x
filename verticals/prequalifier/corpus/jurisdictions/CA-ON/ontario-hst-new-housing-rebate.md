# Ontario HST New Housing Rebate (and 2025/2026 expansions)

The HST applies to most new homes in Ontario at 13% (8% provincial +
5% federal). Several rebate programs partially offset that. This file
covers the established provincial rebate and the major 2025 and 2026
budget expansions.

## Source URLs

- 2025 Fall Economic Statement (HST relief for first-time buyers introduced):
  https://budget.ontario.ca/2025/fallstatement/chapter-1b-costs.html
- 2026 Ontario Budget (HST relief expanded beyond first-time buyers):
  https://budget.ontario.ca/2026/hst.html

## HST framework on new homes (background)

- HST in Ontario on a new home: 13% total.
  - 8% provincial portion.
  - 5% federal portion (the GST).
- HST applies to NEW homes (builder-built or substantially renovated)
  and the federal portion applies to all new home purchases nationally.
- HST generally does NOT apply to resale homes between individuals.

## The existing HST New Housing Rebate

- Provides relief of up to $24,000 on the PROVINCIAL portion for
  eligible purchasers, including non-first-time buyers and
  higher-value property purchases.
- This is the long-standing base rebate that has been in place for
  years and continues to exist.

## 2025 Fall Statement: HST relief for first-time buyers (NEW homes)

Introduced by Ontario in late 2025, mirroring federal proposals:

- Removes the FULL 8% provincial portion of HST for first-time home
  buyers on qualifying NEW homes.
- Applies to new homes valued up to $1 million.
- Relief decreases for higher-valued properties (does not phase out
  abruptly).
- Combined with the federal relief on the same scenario, the full 13%
  HST is removed for qualifying first-time buyers.
- Purchasers must acquire the home as their primary residence.
- Effective for purchase agreements dated May 27, 2025 or later.
- Example given in the source: a first-time buyer on a $1M new home
  receives a combined $80,000 in provincial tax relief from this
  measure plus the existing rebate.

## 2026 Ontario Budget: enhanced rebate expanded beyond first-time buyers

The 2026 budget expanded the relief framework to apply to all eligible
purchasers, not just first-time buyers:

### Enhanced rebate amounts

- Homes valued up to $1M: 100% of the provincial portion, up to
  $80,000 relief.
- Homes valued $1M to $1.5M: capped at $80,000.
- Homes valued $1.5M to $1.85M: relief decreases linearly to $24,000.
- Homes valued above $1.85M: $24,000 baseline (the existing
  long-standing rebate).

This is a significant expansion from the previous $24,000 maximum for
non-first-time buyers.

### Eligibility timelines (homes purchased from builders)

- Purchase agreement signed: April 1, 2026 through March 31, 2027.
- Construction must begin by December 31, 2028.
- Construction must be substantially completed by December 31, 2031.

### Eligibility timelines (owner-built homes)

- Construction must begin: April 1, 2026 through March 31, 2027.
- Construction must be completed by December 31, 2029.

### Rental properties

- Varied deadlines depending on whether construction begins before or
  after March 31, 2026. See source for specifics.

### Implementation status (as of source publication)

- Federal regulations required for rollout.
- Builders cannot initially credit the enhanced rebate at point of
  sale.
- Eligible purchasers apply directly to the Canada Revenue Agency once
  federal regulations are finalized.
- Updated application forms expected post-regulation.

## Practical implications for prequalification

- A first-time buyer purchasing a new home priced at or below $1M can
  expect substantial HST relief (potentially the full 8% provincial
  portion plus matching federal relief, up to roughly $80,000+
  combined).
- A non-first-time buyer of a new home under $1M with a purchase
  agreement from April 1, 2026 onward also benefits from the expanded
  rebate.
- Resale homes (one individual to another) generally have no HST,
  regardless of buyer status.

## What this corpus file is not

- It is not the federal GST/HST New Housing Rebate framework in
  isolation; the federal portion is administered separately by CRA.
- It is not the rebate for substantial renovations; it focuses on new
  builds.
- It is not the rental rebate (NRRP) framework; that has its own
  separate rules.
- It is not the application form or the precise calculation formula
  for the rebate amount; those live with CRA / the Ministry of
  Finance.
- It is not legal or tax advice on a specific transaction.
