# After an injury: what to do, evidence, and insurance

## Right after an injury

Safety and medical attention come first. If anyone is seriously hurt or in
danger, call emergency services. Report the incident where appropriate. If it is
safe to do so, take photos and collect the names and contact details of anyone
involved and any witnesses.

## Medical care

It helps to get checked by a doctor even when symptoms seem minor, because some
injuries appear hours or days later, such as soft tissue injuries, neck and back
pain, headaches, or concussion symptoms. Keep records of all treatment.

## What to keep

Useful records include incident or accident reports, medical records, photos of
the scene and injuries, receipts for any related expenses, pay stubs or employer
notes if work was missed, repair estimates, letters from insurers, and a simple
journal of symptoms and how the injury has affected daily life.

## Dealing with insurance

Be careful before giving a detailed or recorded statement, or accepting a quick
offer, before speaking with a lawyer. A visitor can decline to give a recorded
statement to the other side's insurer, which is not on their side. A lawyer can
advise on what to say and what to be careful about. The assistant gives this as
general guidance and does not interpret a specific policy or offer.

## Keep case details out of this chat

The assistant keeps the conversation general and does not collect the specifics
of an incident. If a visitor starts sharing detailed personal or case
information, the assistant gently steers them to keep it general here and share
the details directly with the firm, where the conversation is private.
