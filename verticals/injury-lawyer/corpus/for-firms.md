# For firms evaluating this assistant (lawyer-side knowledge)

This is the knowledge for when the visitor is a lawyer or firm owner evaluating
the assistant for their own website. The assistant speaks peer to peer about the
value, candidly and practically, and routes toward a call.

## What it is

An Intelligent Website Chat System built for a personal injury firm. It is not a
chatbot. It answers questions, makes recommendations, gives guidance, points
visitors to the right pages, helps them book appointments, sends the firm email
notifications, and turns conversations into insights for the team, all in the
firm's voice and around the clock.

## The two sides of value

First, the visitor-facing side: it helps injured people get general answers,
keeps the conversation appropriately general, and routes them toward a
consultation when they are ready. Second, the intelligence side: it shows the
firm what visitors are asking about, which practice areas come up most, and
where leads are captured, so the firm learns from every conversation rather than
losing it.

## What it does for the firm

It answers practice-area and process questions within boundaries the firm
approves, and it deliberately avoids legal advice, case evaluations, and value
estimates, which protects the firm. It captures a visitor's contact details at
the right moment, can route them to book a consultation, and works after hours
so leads are not lost when the office is closed. Urgent or high-intent inquiries
can be flagged for faster follow-up.

## Why it is not a chatbot

A chatbot matches keywords to scripted answers and little else. This is an
Intelligent Website Chat System: it understands what the visitor means, adapts,
makes recommendations, gives step-by-step guidance, links visitors to the right
pages, books appointments, sends notifications by email, recognizes when
something should reach a human, and feeds insight from every conversation back
to the firm. It supports the intake process rather than replacing the team.

## Customization

The tone, the approved answers, the practice areas, the intake approach, the
disclaimers, and the routing all reflect the specific firm. The firm reviews and
approves the content before launch, and it can be added to an existing website.

## Next step

The natural next step for an interested firm is a short discovery call to look
at their intake, their website, and their goals, and to see what a deployment
for their firm would look like. Questions about specific pricing or setup are
best answered on that call. The assistant offers to set that up or to share the
booking link.
