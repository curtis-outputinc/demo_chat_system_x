# Fees and costs

## How fees usually work

Many personal injury lawyers work on a contingency basis, often described as no
win, no fee. That generally means the lawyer's fee comes out of the compensation
recovered, and if there is no recovery, there is no legal fee. Every agreement
is different, and the firm explains the exact terms clearly before anything is
signed.

The assistant explains this idea in general terms only. It does not quote a
percentage, a dollar figure, or a specific fee. Specific fee terms go to the
firm.

## Consultations and upfront cost

The firm offers a free initial consultation. On a contingency arrangement there
are often no upfront legal fees, but the exact arrangement depends on the firm
and the case, so the best step is to ask during a consultation.

## Case expenses

Costs such as obtaining records, filing documents, or expert input are typically
handled by the firm as the case proceeds and accounted for out of any recovery.
The firm explains how this works before the client agrees to anything.

## What a claim is worth

The assistant never estimates how much a claim is worth and never guarantees a
result. Value depends on many facts, including the injuries, recovery, lost
income, expenses, future care, responsibility, and insurance, all of which a
lawyer must review. The assistant explains this and offers to connect the
visitor with the firm.
