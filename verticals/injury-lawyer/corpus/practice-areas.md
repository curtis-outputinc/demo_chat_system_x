# Practice areas and common situations

For every situation below, the assistant explains in general terms what a lawyer
typically reviews and offers to connect the visitor with the firm. It never
tells a visitor they definitely do or do not have a case.

## Car and auto accidents

Covers rear-end collisions, hit and run, uninsured or underinsured drivers,
injured passengers, and rideshare, taxi, or delivery-vehicle crashes. After a
crash, the priority is safety and medical attention, then documenting the scene
if it is safe, and being careful before giving detailed statements to an
insurer. A lawyer reviews how the crash happened, the injuries, the available
insurance coverage, and the evidence.

## Truck accidents

Crashes involving commercial trucks can be more complex because they may involve
company insurance and safety records. Acting quickly helps preserve records and
evidence. A lawyer can explain who may be involved.

## Motorcycle and cyclist accidents

These often involve serious injuries, road conditions, and driver behavior.
Get medical attention, document the scene if possible, and keep records of
damage. A lawyer reviews the circumstances and available coverage.

## Pedestrian accidents

A person hit while walking should get medical attention, report the incident,
and collect the driver's information if possible. These can be serious cases and
it helps to speak with a lawyer quickly.

## Slip and fall and premises injuries

Property owners and businesses are generally expected to keep their premises
reasonably safe. These cases often depend on what caused the fall, whether the
owner knew or should have known about the hazard, and whether there is evidence
such as photos, an incident report, and witnesses. Common examples include wet
floors, ice and snow, broken or uneven walkways, poor lighting, inadequate
security, unsafe stairs, and falling objects.

## Workplace injuries and workers compensation

Workplace injuries can work differently because a workplace insurance or workers
compensation system may apply instead of a lawsuit. In some situations a
separate claim against a third party (for example, the maker of defective
equipment, or another company on site) may also be possible. Whether someone can
sue an employer, and what benefits apply, depends on the situation and where it
happened. A lawyer can explain which path fits.

## Medical malpractice

These claims generally involve showing that a provider fell below the accepted
standard of care and that this caused harm. They often require expert review and
can have shorter or stricter deadlines, so it helps to speak with a lawyer
promptly.

## Product liability

These involve injuries from defective or dangerous products and can involve the
maker, distributor, or seller. A product recall can be relevant evidence. Keep
the product and any records.

## Dog bites

Get medical attention, identify the owner if possible, report the bite where
appropriate, and keep records of treatment and photos of injuries. Owners are
often responsible for harm caused by their animal.

## Wrongful death

When someone dies because of an injury, certain family members may be able to
bring a claim. Who can do so depends on the circumstances and the law that
applies. A lawyer can explain who may have standing.

## Other situations

Injuries can also happen at gyms, on public transit, at hotels and rentals, at
sporting or recreational events, in parking lots and garages, at amusement parks
and fairs, at schools, on private property, and in situations involving an
intoxicated person. Whether a claim is possible depends on how and where the
injury happened, who was responsible, and whether a waiver was involved. A
lawyer can review the details.
